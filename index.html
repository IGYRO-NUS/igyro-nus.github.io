Program for iGYRO Symposium on Misinformation and Disinformation (iGYRO SMD)

Introduction

iGYRO Symposium on Misinformation and Disinformation

The iGYRO Symposium on Misinformation and Disinformation (iGYRO SMD), organized by the Centre for Trusted Internet Community (CTIC) at the National University of Singapore (NUS), is a pivotal annual event designed to tackle the pervasive issues of misinformation and disinformation in our digital ecosystems. Scheduled for April 24, 2025, this symposium serves as a platform for thought leaders, researchers, and practitioners from diverse fields to come together to enhance understanding, create solutions, and forge partnerships against the backdrop of increasingly complex digital information challenges.

Symposium Objectives

The primary aims of the iGYRO SMD 2025 are to:

  • Elevate the Global Profile of CTIC and iGYRO: Through strategic discussions and the dissemination of cutting-edge research, we aim to reinforce the stature of CTIC and iGYRO as global leaders in the fields of digital information resilience and misinformation management.
  • Encourage Multidisciplinary Collaboration: The symposium is structured to facilitate networking and collaboration among attendees from academia, industry, government, and non-profit sectors, promoting a holistic approach to digital information challenges.
  • Advance Research and Dialogue: By showcasing the latest innovations and fostering discussions across various domains, we strive to steer the direction of future research and policy-making in the realm of digital information management.
  • Recruit and Inspire Future Talent: The symposium also aims to attract and inspire new talents by highlighting the dynamic research environment and the impactful work being conducted at NUS and CTIC.

Symposium Format

The day-long event will be held in the i4.0 Seminar Room and will feature a mix of keynote speeches, oral presentations, and panel discussions, punctuated with Q&A sessions to encourage interaction and dialogue. Key themes will be addressed by distinguished speakers, including international experts and leaders in related fields.

The symposium will also include poster sessions and live demonstrations, providing a hands-on look at the latest tools and studies in the fight against misinformation.

Organizing Committee

The organizing committee, chaired by Prof. Mong Li and led by Programme Lead Fei Hao, with logistical support from Wendy, has curated a program that not only addresses the theoretical underpinnings of misinformation but also explores practical solutions and innovative technologies being developed to combat it.

Join Us

We invite scholars, industry experts, policy makers, and students to join us at this important event, contributing to a future where digital information is more trustworthy and society is better equipped to combat misinformation and disinformation.
